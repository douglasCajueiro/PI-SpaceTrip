Destination Mars

Located more than 54 million kilometers away from Earth, the so-called "Red Planet" may be your next stop. The moons Phobos and Deimos, the polar colotas, Mount Olympus (largest volcano in the solar system) and Ma'adim Vallis (one of the largest canyons on Mars) are part of this adventure. Embark on this incredible journey of knowledge!

Package all-inclusive (240 days): 8 x $200,000

Destination Earth Orbit

Come with us on a relaxing and quick trip around the earth. We will contemplate our magnificent planet, with all its diversity and beauty. Be sure to take lots of pictures and admire the points of lights at night. On this trip we will have the possibility to make discoveries, like new islands in our immense blue marble planet.

Package all-inclusive (60 days): 1 x $230,000

Destination Moon

Did you know that if we didn't have the moon, our days would last 6 hours? In this magical journey, we will unravel its mysteries and learn a little more about our only natural satellite and closest celestial neighbor. We will see the first footprints of man on the moon, where the American flags demarcated territory and also, a route between the craters and the seas without water. Next stop, Moon!

Package all-inclusive (90 days): 2 x $190,000

Destination Saturn

Seen from Earth, Saturn appears to be a bright star in the sky, easily visible. But, make no mistake, this beautiful planet is 1.4 billion km from Earth. In this itinerary we will have beautiful polar dawns, clouds of different shades, the famous Rings of Saturn and also a visit to one of its incredible moons, the so-called Titan, which has been studied by scientists due to the existence of lakes. So, do not waste time and come with us in this dream destination!

Package all-inclusive (2 years): 24 x $250,000

Destination Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System. And in this long journey we will discover all its mysteries. For adventurers this is a great option, as we will know the incredible "Great Red Spot" and the satellites discovered by Galileo Galilei in 1610, one of them called Europa, which has a liquid ocean covered with ice. Will our next planet be habitable? Come and find out!

Package all-inclusive (1 year): 12 x $190,000

Destination Venus

It was named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty Venus. After the moon, it is the brightest object in the night sky. In addition to the impact craters, mountains and valleys commonly found on rocky planets, Venus brings together a set of unique geographical features. Among these we will explore volcanoes with flat tops, starry radial fracture systems and landforms with radial and concentric fractures resembling cobwebs. Located just over 40 million km away, Venus becomes a pleasant ride for first-time passengers.

Package all-inclusive (200 days): 6 x $100,000
